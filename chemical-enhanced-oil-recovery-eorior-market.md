<p><p>Chemical Enhanced Oil Recovery (EOR/IOR) involves the injection of chemicals into oil reservoirs to improve oil extraction efficiency beyond what is achievable through conventional methods. This technique is designed to alter the properties of oil and the reservoir environment, leading to increased oil mobility and recovery rates. The chemicals commonly used include surfactants, polymers, and alkaline agents, which help in reducing interfacial tension, altering viscosity, and mobilizing trapped oil.</p><p>The Chemical Enhanced Oil Recovery (EOR/IOR) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.4% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by rising global energy demand, the need for sustainable production techniques, and the continual depletion of easily accessible oil reserves. Moreover, advancements in formulation technology and increased investment in research and development are enhancing the effectiveness and cost-efficiency of these chemical processes. Trends indicate a greater focus on environmentally friendly and low-cost solutions, alongside the integration of digital technologies to optimize EOR operations. The market is also witnessing a shift towards unconventional oil sources, propelling the adoption of innovative chemical recovery techniques to maintain production levels in aging fields.</p></p>
