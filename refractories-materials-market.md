<p><p>Refractory materials are heat-resistant substances used in high-temperature applications like furnaces and kilns. They are categorized into acidic, neutral, and basic types based on their chemical composition and resistance to specific environments. **Acidic refractories**, made from silica or alumina-silica, resist acidic slags but degrade in alkaline conditions. **Neutral refractories**, like alumina or chromite, withstand both acidic and basic environments. **Basic refractories**, composed of magnesia or dolomite, excel in alkaline conditions but are vulnerable to acidic reactions. Each type is chosen based on the operational environment and material compatibility.</p></p>
